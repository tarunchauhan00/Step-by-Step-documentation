    6. How to Use (User Guide)¶

    Below is a step-by-step guide, separated by user role.

    Owner/Admin Workflow¶

    1. Sign Up / Log In

      • First time: click Sign Up, complete registration, then log in.
      • Visit https://ffms.netlify.app/.
      • Returning users: log in directly.

    2. View Processes

      • After login, you land on /processes.
      • If you have no processes yet, you’ll see an empty state with an Add Process button.

    3. Create a Process

      • Click Add Process → you see fields for:
      • Process Name (text)
      • Editor Emails (comma-separated, for any co-owners or editors)
      • Form Type (Buttons: Google Form or Crafty Form)
      • If you choose Google Form: clicking Save will trigger a backend call to create a new Google Form via your Netlify function (using your stored googleToken). Once created, the app saves google_form_id and google_form_url in the database, then opens the Google Form editor in a new tab.
      • If you choose Crafty Form: clicking Save opens the Crafty Form builder in a new tab. After you finish building, paste the shared form link back into the Crafty Form URL field. Then click Save again to store the form_url in Supabase.
      • After the form is associated (Google or Crafty), you’re redirected to /process/<newProcessId>/steps.

    4. Open a Process

      • Click any Process card → you go to /process/<processId>/steps (the StepCreation page).
      • If no steps exist yet, you’ll see “No steps yet” plus an Add Step button.

    5. Add a Single Step

      • On /process/<processId>/steps, click Add Step → you arrive at the AddStep form.
      • Fill in:
      • Step Name & Description (text fields)
      • Due Date (e.g., June 15, 2025)
      • Assign to Doer (dropdown; only users with role = doer appear)
      • (Optional) Google Form URL
      • (Optional) Custom Form (if you have already built one under Forms)
      • Click Save. The new Step appears in the list.

    6. Bulk Edit / Reorder Steps

      • If you have more than one Step, click Bulk Edit (in the top bar of StepCreation). That opens the BulkAddFlowchart page.
      • On BulkAddFlowchart, you see all existing Steps in a list with their Step ID, Sequence ID, TAT, name, and responsibility.
      • To add a parallel Step next to any existing one, click the small + icon on the right side of that Step’s card. (Parallel means same sequenceId, stepId + 1.)
      • To add a sequential Step directly below any existing one, click the + button just beneath that Step. (Sequential means both stepId and sequenceId increment by 1.)
      • To edit an existing Step, click the three‐dot menu (⋮) on that Step and choose Edit—this takes you to the AddStep form prefilled with that Step’s data.
      • To delete an existing Step, click the three‐dot menu and choose Delete. You’ll be prompted to confirm.
      • After adding as many new Steps as needed, click Save All at the top. This will batch‐insert only the newly added Steps (it automatically re‐sequences them before insertion).
      • Or click Cancel to return without saving.
      • After Save All, you return to /process/<processId>/steps, and the new Steps appear in the Step list.

    7. Send Reminders

      • Visit Reminders (/reminders).
      • You’ll see lists of Steps that are “Due Soon” or “Overdue.”
      • Click Send Reminder next to any Step → triggers an email or in-app reminder (depending on your Supabase SMTP setup).
      • You can also choose “Send to All Overdue” or “Send to All Due Today” to batch‐send.

    8. View Reports

      • Visit Reports (/report). The ReportPage offers two main views:
      • By Process (Timeline Mode)
        • Shows each Process as a vertical timeline entry containing:
        • Date Cell: the earliest start time among that Process’s Steps (with a clock icon).
        • Progress Card:
          • Process name and a collapse/expand chevron.
          • Three colored tags showing:
          • Total Steps (e.g., “5 Steps”)
          • Total Data Points (number of form submissions)
          • Total TAT (sum of all Steps’ TAT, in hours)
          • A horizontal progress bar indicating the percentage of Steps completed (e.g., “3 / 5 steps (60%)”).
          • When expanded, an indented list of all Steps in that Process, each showing:
          • Status Pill (Pending, In Progress, or Completed—with distinct background/foreground colors).
          • Start Time (formatted “YYYY-MM-DD HH:mm:ss” in IST) or “—” if not started.
          • Duration (“Xh Ym”) from started_at to completed_at or to now, and if overdue, “• Overdue Xh Ym.”
        • Clicking the chevron toggles showing/hiding that Process’s step list.
      • By Step (List Mode)
        • Flattens every Step across all Processes into one scrollable list. Each Step item shows:
        • “ProcessName: StepName” (bold), followed by a status pill.
        • An arrow button → clicking it switches back to the timeline view and scrolls that Process card into center.
        • Status Sequence (if defined), e.g. “Approved → Completed,” or “No statuses.”
        • Start Time (or “—”) and Duration/Overdue info.
      • Sorting Controls (at top of the page):
      • Two buttons: “Oldest first” and “Newest first.” These toggle how the Processes (and their Steps) are ordered.
      • Loading State: while data is loading, you see “Loading…”.
      • Use the toggle buttons above the timeline or list to switch between “By Process” and “By Step” modes.

    Doer/Contributor Workflow¶

    1. View Assigned Steps

      • After login, go to /doer/steps (DoerStepList).
      • You’ll see all active (pending or in-progress) Steps assigned to you. Each card shows Step name, TAT, and any existing status or data points.

    2. Search, Filter, Sort, and Group

      • Search Box: type part of a Step name to filter the list.
      • Filter Menu (click the filter icon):
      • All Steps
      • TAT < 10 hrs
      • TAT ≥ 10 hrs
      • Sort Menu (click the sort icon):
      • No Sort
      • TAT Ascending
      • TAT Descending
      • Sequence Ascending
      • Sequence Descending
      • Group Menu (click the group icon):
      • None (flat list)
      • Process (group cards under collapsible “Process: [Name]” headers)
      • Data Points (group cards by sequence_id under “Sequence: [ID]” headers)

    3. Open a Step

      • Click on any Step card → you go to /doer/step/<stepId>/submission/<submissionId> (DoerStepDetail).
      • If the Step uses a Crafty Form, you’ll see a form transposed view.
      • If the Step uses a Google Form, you’ll see the form transposed view.

    4. Fill & Submit

      • Complete all required fields, then click Submit.
      • The app stores your answers in form_responses and updates the Step’s status to completed.
      • You are redirected to /doer/submission/<submissionId> (DoerSubmissionDetail).

    5. View Submission Details

      • On /doer/submission/<submissionId>, you see a read-only view of each question and your answers, with timestamps.
      • You cannot edit after submission—this is strictly a detail view.

    6. Receive Reminders

      • If an Admin sends you a reminder, you receive either:
      • An email (if SMTP is configured), or
      • An in-app alert (if the Admin used the ReminderSection).
